  • In Lewisham, the case rate for people age 60 and over was lower than the overall case rate, at a level of 424.8 per 100,000 residents. This means that people of age 60 and over in Lewisham were less likely to be infected than the overall population of the borough.

What do these numbers mean and why is the case rate important?

The case rate allows us to compare infection levels between places that have very different population sizes. It is calculated as the total number of COVID-19 cases in a region over seven days, divided by the population size of the region.
